> Abstract

![Love in the Big City](/img/publications/LoveintheBigCity.png)

Adapting a contemporary Korean novel across different media is more than a shift in form — it reveals transformations in meaning shaped by socio-cultural context. This thesis comparatively analyzes how Sang Young Park’s “Love in the Big City (2019)”, particularly its first episode ‘Jaehee’ (or ‘Miae’ in the drama), is reinterpreted through three media in contemporary contexts: the original novel, film (2024), and drama series (2024). Moving beyond traditional fidelity discourse, the study employs adaptation theory to analyze how each version constructs its own emotional and narrative logic within distinct socio-cultural frameworks. Through close analysis of narrative structures, character portrayals, thematic shifts, and directorial approaches, the research investigates how queer identity and the core message are mediated, transformed, and re-signified across media.
