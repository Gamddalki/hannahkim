- From Digital Exclusion to Inclusive Connection

Transforming the **stressful experience** of the digital ordering kiosk into an **accessible, intuitive voice-guided journey** that fosters digital inclusion for middle-aged and older adults, empowering them to break through digital barriers with their voice.
:::

> Motivation

As **contactless kiosks** become ubiquitous, many middle-aged and older adults struggle with their **complex interfaces, low readability, and lack of guidance**. To address these accessibility barriers, our team designed “Jeogiyo” — a voice-enabled assistant application that streamlines digital ordering through conversational **Voice User Interface (VUI)**.

![Kiosk barrier research](/img/projects/Jeogiyo2.png)

> Approach

We conducted a survey (N=28), which confirmed that **users over the age of 50 struggled significantly** with current contactless systems (e.g. kiosk). Based on these insights, we conceptualized and prototyped a VUI system where ordering could be initiated and guided entirely through natural voice commands.

![User flow architecture](/img/projects/Jeogiyo3.png)

We designed the interaction model and implemented **fallback mechanisms** (simplified navigation and optional typing) to maximize inclusivity. My contributions spanned concept development, user research, and interaction design. I formulated the conversational architecture, integrated accessibility principles into the interface, and defined system requirements to ensure smooth, low-effort operation across devices.

![Prototype application screens](/img/projects/Jeogiyo4.png)

> Outcomes

- **Validated accessibility needs through survey-driven insights**.
- Designed simple and intuitive UI/UX elements to lower digital barriers, demonstrating how voice interaction can reduce friction and anxiety in digital ordering for middle-aged and older adults.
- Delivered a prototype of a mobile application with VUI.
- Received positive peer feedback for bridging generational gaps in digital usability.

> Reflection

This project deepened my understanding of accessible interaction design and the importance of multimodal interfaces that cater to diverse user groups. It reinforced my **interest in developing human-centered technologies** that reduce digital inequality and empower broader participation.
