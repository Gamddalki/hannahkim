Incentivizing **ice pack recycling** through a user-friendly mobile application connected to real-world collection bins.

> Motivation

With the surge in home deliveries **during the COVID-19 pandemic**, the use of ice packs for refrigerated and frozen goods increased dramatically, leading to a buildup of unused packs in households. Due to their superabsorbent polymer content, which is a form of microplastic, ice packs are difficult to recycle and often end up as environmentally harmful waste. Existing collection efforts such as temporary municipal events proved too limited in scale and continuity.

![Pandemic delivery growth](/img/projects/reICEcle2.png)

Recognizing this gap using the **5 Whys framework**, our team aimed to design a sustainable, reward-based recycling system that motivates households to return used ice packs while supporting local small businesses affected by the pandemic.

> Approach

We conducted a **user survey (N=42)** to capture household recycling behaviors. Insights guided reward system design and interface priorities.

![User Survey Insights](/img/projects/reICEcle3.png)

Based on the user study, we designed ReICEcle, a prototype mobile application that encourages households to **recycle used ice packs** through a point-based reward system. The system integrates a physical collection bin equipped with weight measurement, enabling users to deposit ice packs, track their contributions, and redeem points at local businesses affected by COVID-19.

Our approach combined **software engineering methodologies** (e.g. UML modeling) and prototyping to meet user requirements. As **team leader**, I led the project from problem definition to final prototype delivery, overseeing both the design process and system integration.

![UML Diagram & Prototyping](/img/projects/reICEcle4.png)
