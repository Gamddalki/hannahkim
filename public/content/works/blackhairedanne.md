![Key Concept](/img/projects/BlackHairedAnne1.png)

---

Twisting a well-known novel with a playful ‘what-if’: Did you know that Anne was **actually black haired**? Maybe you did, but no one in the village should.
:::

> Motivation

:::col-split
The project originated from a club ideation session, where we each had to develop a game concept inspired by the color red. I proposed a unique 'what-if' that inverted the classic story of “Anne of Green Gables”: what if Anne wasn't born with red hair, but **secretly dyed it to maintain her iconic look**? After my presentation, several teammates reached out to collaborate, and what began as a concept presentation grew into my first-ever development project.

---

![Ideation process](/img/projects/BlackHairedAnne2.png)

:::

> Approach

As a team leader and a game designer, I developed the storyline and **narrative mechanics**, designed the UI flow, created screen layouts and items.

:::col-split
![UI layout design](/img/projects/BlackHairedAnne3.png)

---

![Sprite animations](/img/projects/BlackHairedAnne4.png)
:::

I also created **frame-by-frame sprite animations** using Aseprite to achieve fluid running motion. In sound design, I tried to ensure that the game’s atmosphere aligned with its playful narrative.

Our team collaboratively refined the **rumor gauge system** and continuously tested installations across devices to ensure a stable, fully functional APK. Each iteration improved gameplay balance and performance, transforming an imaginative pitch into a complete mobile experience.

> Outcomes

- Delivered a fully functional mobile game (playable on Android devices via APK installation) built from original concept to completion.
- Designed and implemented UI, character animations, items, and in-game sound.
- Developed in collaboration through iterative testing and balancing.
- **Awarded 2nd Place** for creativity and a high level of polish within the club.

> Reflection

This project marked my first full development cycle, transforming an idea into a finished, functioning mobile game. Because I was unfamiliar with the technical requirements of Unity and the process of game design, I had to repeatedly revise the design — especially in map structure and feature specifications. This experience taught me the importance of technically grounded design, motivating me to strengthen my computer science foundation.
