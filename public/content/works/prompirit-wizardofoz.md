> Motivation

The project was driven by the need to validate the improvement in user experience offered by the **“Prompirit” prompt engineering pipeline**. While statistical analysis and a prior user survey confirmed the pipeline’s strength, we conducted this follow-up interview study to gain deeper insights into user perceptions. Designed to overcome the common **shortcomings of existing models** (poor emotional fidelity, weak context comprehension), our primary objective was to measure the pipeline's effectiveness through authentic user interaction and feedback.

> Approach

We conducted an **IRB-approved user study (N=18)** utilizing the **Wizard of Oz (WoZ) prototyping technique** to simulate the 'Prompirit' pipeline in a real-life application scenario.

The interview structure was meticulously designed for comprehensive data collection:

1.  **Baseline & Task**: Participants interacted with three tools (DALL·E, Stable Diffusion, and the Prompirit prototype) using pre-defined prompts. A **think-aloud protocol** was used to capture real-time thoughts on overall user experience.
2.  **Comparative Analysis**: The core task consisted of **three sessions** (covering both positive and negative emotions), where participants compared images from all three systems based on **alignment with text, aesthetic appeal, and emotional fidelity**. Each session was concluded by choosing the best tool.
3.  **Final Assessment**: The interview concluded with a structured interview gauging perceived problem-solving efficacy, intent for future use, necessary improvements, and potential application fields.

As the main interviewer for 9 sessions, I could gain direct qualitative insight. I also designed and built the **Firebase-hosted prototype website** used for the interviews, ensuring a seamless and bias-free experience.

The study provided robust validation for the 'Prompirit' pipeline, confirming its superior user experience. Although not included in the published IEEE paper, this follow-up study provided meaningful insights into user-centered evaluation of generative AI and demonstrated the feasibility of prototype-based fair user testing.

> Outcomes

- Established a clear user preference, with 'Prompirit' **winning 34 of the 54 comparative sessions** (DALL·E: 20, Stable Diffusion: 0).
- Validated the pipeline's problem-solving efficacy, confirmed by **66.7% of participants** (12 out of 18) who attested that 'Prompirit' successfully solved key shortcomings in existing image generation tools.
- Demonstrated high market viability, achieving **100% user adoption intent** (10 Agree, 8 Strongly Agree) for daily life use.
- Identified qualitative strengths through **thematic analysis** that highlighted superior emotional expressiveness, stylistic consistency, and contextual comprehension as primary drivers for user preference.

> Reflection

This project provided invaluable experience in executing ethical, technically complex user research within the constraints of an IRB-approved protocol. It reinforced my belief that technological innovation must be validated through empathetic, user-centered methodologies. The success of the WoZ prototyping method, which I designed and deployed, cemented my skill in designing research tools that bridge the gap between the concept and authentic human interaction, significantly strengthening my ability to build human-centered AI systems in future research.
