> Motivation

Developed during the 2023 EPITA Summer IT Program, the core motivation was to explore the nuances of **VR-based interaction** and **spatial puzzle design**. The project, “Vivid Nightmare”, aimed to build more than just a typical escape room; it sought to immerse players in a surreal dream world where escalating time pressure and bizarre obstacles would dramatically intensify the emotional stakes of the escape.

> Approach

I took the lead on game design, level structure, and interaction mechanics. We designed three levels, each with a **progressively complex key-finding mechanic** and environmental hazards. The narrative progression culminated in an ending scene, where the alarm rings, that was designed for the successful accomplishment of all stages.

![VR escape level interface](/img/projects/VividNightmare2.png)

To ensure replayability, keys were randomized at runtime, and the wrist-based timer was used to provide intuitive gameplay feedback.

Through iterative playtesting, we refined obstacle pacing, visibility, and spatial awareness within the VR environment to deliver a truly engaging and immersive experience.

> Outcomes

- Delivered a fully developed VR game with a strong narrative.
- Showcased the project as a highlight of the 2023 EPITA Summer IT Program, receiving praise from players and professors for the **balance of tension, challenge, and immersive design**.

> Reflection

This project significantly strengthened my skills in VR interaction design and immersive storytelling. It taught me that **small interface details**, like the wrist-mounted timer, can profoundly affect player engagement and tension. The experience strongly reinforced my interest in combining narrative design and interactive technology to create impactful experiences.
