> Key Insights

- From Plain Text Information to Immersive AR Experience

:::col-split
![Key Concept](/img/projects/Senior'sSus1.png)

---

Transforming **bland, forgettable benefits** of the student ID card into an **engaging, story-driven AR game**, helping freshmen discover and remember the hidden campus life tips.
:::

> Motivation

While student ID cards are often seen as simple access tools, they actually unlock a wide range of campus services — from device rental services to various discounts. However, this information is often delivered as **plain text** during orientation, making it **forgettable and underutilized**.

![Campus ID benefits analysis](/img/projects/Senior'sSus2.png)

> Approach

To address this, our team developed “Senior’s Suspicious Diary”, a mobile AR mystery game that redesigns how campus information is delivered to freshmen. We **translated each tip directly into a physical campus location** through AR-based interactive exploration. As players follow the traces of a senior’s diary across key campus landmarks such as the ECC Art House, Central Library, Chapel, and Engineering Building, they uncover both **narrative clues** and **practical student ID card tips** through puzzles and AR recognition.

![AR gameplay flow](/img/projects/Senior'sSus3.png)

While contributing equally to development, I conceptualized and orchestrated the overall narrative and gameplay flow, designed intuitive mission interfaces inspired by familiar student apps, and crafted immersive sound design to enhance the eerie tone. Our collaborative team iterated, tested, and refined the gameplay together, ensuring a fully functional and engaging experience.

> Outcomes

- Designed and implemented core UI/UX elements to optimize user flow and maintain consistent visual identity.
- Delivered a fully functional mobile AR application that integrates storytelling, location-based tasks, and interactive puzzles.
- Enhanced immersion through sound design and collaborative debugging during field testing.
- Received positive feedback from faculty particularly with the narrative structure and the concept of immersive storytelling.

> Reflection

This project bridged UI/UX design, narrative development, and AR technology — allowing me to experiment with **storytelling as a tool for abstract educational needs**. It reinforced my ability to translate plain text information into interactive, human-centered experiences. The positive feedback from both faculty and classmates, especially regarding the narrative and tone, reaffirmed the potential of storytelling as a design tool, **not just for entertainment but for engagement and retention**.
